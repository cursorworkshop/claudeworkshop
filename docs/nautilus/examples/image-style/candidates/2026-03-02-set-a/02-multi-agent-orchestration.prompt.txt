# Reference Style Spec (Desk Sketch v2)

This file is the single source of truth for the hero-image style.  
Goal: match the reference look as closely as possible for every generated research image.

Reference asset: `target-style-reference.png` (800x600, 4:3).

## 1) Visual Identity

- Medium: hand-drawn pen sketch (ballpoint / fineliner vibe), not paint or vector.
- Ink color: one dark blue-navy line color family only.
- Rendering intent: fast editorial notebook drawing, intentionally unfinished.
- Overall feel: human-made, rough, observational sketch.

## 2) Subject & Camera

- Exactly one person, seated, actively working at a desk.
- Viewpoint: over-shoulder / rear three-quarter side profile.
- Subject placement: right half of frame.
- Monitor placement: left half of frame.
- Typical silhouette: hoodie + glasses + office chair contour.
- Keep anatomy believable but not hyper-detailed.

## 3) Scene Grammar

- Exactly one desk setup and one monitor.
- Optional props: keyboard, simple lamp, minimal desk lines.
- No secondary narrative panels, no collage, no cutaways.
- Large negative space is required.

## 4) Linework DNA

- Stroke quality: jittery, broken, pressure-varying, sketchy.
- Priority order:
  1) expressive contour lines
  2) local scribble hatching
  3) sparse construction lines
- Keep hatch density low-to-medium; leave visible paper/negative-space gaps.
- Keep rough overlaps and slight perspective imperfections.
- Avoid smooth vector-like contours and polished edge cleanup.
- Avoid heavy global crosshatching; shading should stay local.

## 5) Tonal & Color DNA

- Background: warm peach/salmon paper tone.
- Gradient: optional, but very subtle and low contrast.
- Contrast strategy: dark ink subject over soft warm background.
- No saturated neon accents, no multicolor ink.

## 6) Monitor Content Rules

- The monitor can include sparse pseudo-code-like marks and rough short lines.
- Prefer sketchy, low-fidelity marks over polished UI components.
- Semi-legible tiny text-like marks are acceptable if they stay sketchy and non-branded.
- Never include logos, badges, product branding, or watermark text.
- Avoid heavy box-arrow flowchart structures as dominant screen content.

## 7) Hard Negatives (Must Never Appear)

- Additional people or faces.
- Photorealism, 3D rendering, glossy shading, comic-book polish.
- Highly clean geometric/vector finish.
- Readable text, logos, watermarks, signatures, branding.
- Floating arrows/icons/callouts outside the monitor.
- Futuristic UI overlays around the person.

## 8) Pass/Fail Checklist

An image should fail if any of these are true:

- It looks "designed" or "rendered" instead of quickly sketched.
- The screen content reads like a crisp diagram or polished UI.
- The linework is too uniform, too clean, or too perfect.
- The background is not warm peach/salmon or is too visually busy.

An image should pass only if first impression is:

> "A rough notebook pen sketch of one developer at a desk, on warm paper."

## 9) Prompting Notes

- Use direct style-lock language ("rough", "unfinished", "jittery strokes").
- Repeat one-person / one-monitor constraints.
- Repeat "no readable text" and "no floating symbols outside monitor".
- Ask for sketch-first outcome, not polished illustration.
