Create a 16:9 editorial hero illustration for a technical research article.

Style lock:
- Rough notebook pen sketch (ballpoint / fineliner feel), single dark navy ink.
- Intentionally unfinished and human: jittery strokes, small inaccuracies, visible construction lines.
- Local scribble hatching only (hair/hoodie/chair); avoid heavy global shading.
- Keep hatching density low-to-medium; preserve visible paper whitespace.
- Must look like a quick observational drawing, not polished concept art.
- Warm peach/salmon paper background; near-flat tone, gradient only if very subtle and smooth.
- Background must feel clean and calm, not grainy, fibrous, mottled, or distressed.
- Avoid visible paper texture, speckle, watercolor bloom, or print-like noise in the background.

Composition lock:
- Exactly one focal subject or focal object.
- Human is optional.
- No computers/monitors/laptops/screens by default.
- Do not default to "person behind computer".
- Prefer abstract, high-level conceptual depiction over literal interface screenshots.
- Keep subject/object smaller (roughly 25-35% canvas area).
- Keep large negative space (roughly 45-60% empty paper/desk/background).
- Keep generous breathing room with minimal supporting props.

Screen lock:
- Avoid monitors/screens entirely unless explicitly required.
- No readable text, words, letters, numbers, labels, UI copy, or logos anywhere in the image.
- No dominant box-arrow flowcharts.

Hard negatives:
- No extra people.
- No computer/monitor/laptop/screen/tablet/phone/browser/terminal visuals.
- No floating arrows/icons/callouts.
- No readable text or labels anywhere.
- No photorealism, 3D, glossy rendering, cartoon/comic style.
- No watermark, signature, or branding anywhere.

Quality bar:
- At thumbnail size, first impression should be:
  "rough hand-sketched technical editorial scene on a clean pastel field."
